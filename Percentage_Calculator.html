About Percentages

What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. It is often denoted using the percent sign, "%". For example, 45% is equal to 45/100, or 0.45.

Basic Percentage Formula:

Percentage = (Value / Total Value) × 100

Common Percentage Calculations

  • Percentage of a number: What is 15% of 200? (15/100) × 200 = 30
  • Percentage change: What is the percentage increase from 50 to 75? [(75-50)/50] × 100 = 50%
  • Percentage proportion: 25 is what percent of 200? (25/200) × 100 = 12.5%

Real-World Applications

Percentages are used in various real-life situations:

  • Finance: Calculating interest rates, discounts, and investments
  • Statistics: Representing data and survey results
  • Science: Expressing concentrations, success rates, and changes
  • Daily life: Calculating tips, taxes, and sale prices

Percentage Increase Formula:

Percentage Increase = [(New Value - Original Value) / Original Value] × 100

Percentage Decrease Formula:

Percentage Decrease = [(Original Value - New Value) / Original Value] × 100

Tips for Working with Percentages

  • To find 10% of a number, simply move the decimal point one place to the left
  • To find 1% of a number, move the decimal point two places to the left
  • To increase a number by a percentage, multiply by (1 + percentage/100)
  • To decrease a number by a percentage, multiply by (1 - percentage/100)
