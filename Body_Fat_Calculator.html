About Body Fat Percentage

What is Body Fat Percentage?

Body fat percentage is the total mass of fat divided by total body mass, multiplied by 100. It includes both essential fat (needed for normal bodily functions) and storage fat.

Unlike BMI, body fat percentage distinguishes between fat mass and lean mass, providing a more accurate picture of health and fitness.

Health Implications

Maintaining a healthy body fat percentage is important for:

  • Hormone regulation: Essential fat helps regulate hormones
  • Organ protection: Fat cushions and protects internal organs
  • Temperature regulation: Fat helps maintain body temperature
  • Energy storage: Fat stores energy for future use

Too little body fat can lead to hormonal imbalances, while too much increases risk for heart disease, diabetes, and other health problems.

Calculation Methods

U.S. Navy Method: Uses body circumference measurements. While not as accurate as DEXA scans or hydrostatic weighing, it's accessible and provides a reasonable estimate.

BMI Method: Uses Body Mass Index to estimate body fat percentage. Less accurate but useful when circumference measurements aren't available.
