Activity Guide

What are METs?

MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) is a unit that estimates the amount of energy used by the body during physical activity, compared to resting metabolism. 1 MET is the energy you spend while at rest.

Calories Burned = MET × Weight (kg) × Time (hours)

For example: A 70 kg person walking (MET 3.5) for 1 hour burns:
3.5 × 70 × 1 = 245 calories

Daily Calorie Burn Goals

For weight maintenance, aim to burn 200-400 calories through exercise daily. For weight loss, aim for 400-600+ calories daily, combined with a healthy diet.
