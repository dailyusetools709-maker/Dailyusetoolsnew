About BMR

What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to accomplish its most basic (basal) life-sustaining functions at complete rest. This includes breathing, circulation, cell production, nutrient processing, protein synthesis, and ion transport.

Why is BMR Important?

Understanding your BMR is crucial for:

  • Weight management: Knowing how many calories your body burns at rest helps you create effective diet plans
  • Nutrition planning: Helps determine appropriate calorie intake for your goals
  • Health assessment: Can indicate thyroid problems or metabolic issues if significantly higher or lower than expected
  • Fitness goals: Essential for calculating calorie needs for muscle gain or fat loss

How BMR is Calculated

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor Equation, which is considered the most accurate BMR formula for the general population:

  • For men: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) + 5
  • For women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) - 5 × age(y) - 161

Your Daily Calorie Needs are calculated by multiplying your BMR by an activity factor that matches your lifestyle.

Note: BMR is only an estimate. Individual variations in muscle mass, body fat percentage, genetics, and health conditions can affect your actual metabolic rate. Consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice.
