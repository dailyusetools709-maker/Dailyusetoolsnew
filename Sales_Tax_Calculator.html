About Sales Tax

What is Sales Tax?

Sales tax is a consumption tax imposed by the government on the sale of goods and services. It's usually calculated as a percentage of the purchase price and collected by the seller at the point of sale.

How to Calculate Sales Tax

The formula for calculating sales tax is simple:

Sales Tax = Price × (Tax Rate / 100)
Total Price = Price + Sales Tax

For multiple items:
Total Tax = (Price × Quantity) × (Tax Rate / 100)

Combined Tax Rates

In many locations, the total sales tax rate is a combination of:

  • State sales tax
  • County sales tax
  • City or municipal sales tax
  • Special district taxes

For example, in some California cities, the total tax rate might be: State (7.25%) + County (1%) + City (1.5%) = 9.75% total.

Tax-Exempt Items

Some items are often exempt from sales tax, including:

  • Groceries (in most states)
  • Prescription drugs
  • Clothing (in some states)
  • Educational materials
