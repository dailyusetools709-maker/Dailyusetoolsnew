About Ideal Weight

What is Ideal Weight?

Ideal body weight is a measurement that defines what your weight should be based on your height, gender, and body frame. It's not a one-size-fits-all number but a range that correlates with the lowest risk of health issues.

Formulas Used

  • Robinson Formula
    Developed in 1983, based on Metropolitan Life Insurance data
  • Miller Formula
    Created in 1983 as an update to the Devine formula
  • Hamwi Formula
    Developed in 1964 for medicinal dosage calculations
  • Devine Formula
    Created in 1974 for medical use, very commonly used

How to Interpret Results

Your ideal weight is a range rather than a single number. The calculator provides results from four different scientific formulas, giving you a comprehensive view of what your weight should be for optimal health.

Consider your body composition, muscle mass, and individual health factors when interpreting these results. Always consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice.

Important Note

This calculator provides estimates based on statistical formulas. Individual factors like muscle mass, bone density, and overall health condition can affect your ideal weight. These results should be used as a guideline, not a medical diagnosis.
