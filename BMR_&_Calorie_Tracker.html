About BMR

What is BMR?

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the number of calories your body needs to accomplish its most basic (basal) life-sustaining functions, including:

  • Breathing
  • Circulation
  • Cell production
  • Nutrient processing
  • Protein synthesis

This accounts for about 60-70% of the calories you burn each day.

BMR Formulas

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (Most Accurate):
Men: (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age in years) + 5
Women: (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age in years) - 161
Harris-Benedict Equation (Original):
Men: 66.5 + (13.75 × weight in kg) + (5.003 × height in cm) - (6.755 × age in years)
Women: 655.1 + (9.563 × weight in kg) + (1.850 × height in cm) - (4.676 × age in years)
Katch-McArdle Equation (Requires body fat %):
370 + (21.6 × lean body mass in kg)
Lean body mass = weight × (100 - body fat %) / 100

How to Use Your Results

Weight Maintenance: Consume the maintenance calories to keep your current weight.

Weight Loss: Consume 500 calories less than maintenance per day to lose about 0.5 kg (1 lb) per week.

Weight Gain: Consume 500 calories more than maintenance per day to gain about 0.5 kg (1 lb) per week.

Important Note

These calculations are estimates. Individual variations in metabolism, genetics, and health conditions can affect your actual calorie needs. Consult with a healthcare professional before making significant dietary changes.
